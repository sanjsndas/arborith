Cookies & quiet data

We use cookies sparingly, like compost — only to enrich your experience, never to exploit. Here’s what we do and why.

Our approach to cookies

At Arborith, we believe in transparency. We use minimal cookies to keep the site functional, understand broad usage patterns, and remember your preferences. We never sell your data or use invasive tracking.

🌱 Essential cookies — these keep the site running (e.g., session management, security). No consent needed, as they are strictly necessary.

🌿 Preference cookies — remember your choices (e.g., newsletter pop‑up dismissal, theme).

🍃 Analytics cookies — we use a privacy‑first, self‑hosted analytics tool (no IP tracking, no cross‑site tracking).

What we do not use

  • No third‑party advertising cookies
  • No fingerprinting or persistent identifiers
  • No cross‑site tracking or retargeting

Your choices

You can manage cookie preferences in your browser settings. Blocking essential cookies may affect site functionality, but we respect your decision. We also offer a simple “cookie consent” banner on first visit — you can accept or decline non‑essential cookies.

Third‑party links

Occasionally we link to external resources (e.g., botanical archives, partners). Those sites have their own cookie policies, and we encourage you to review them.

Changes to this policy

We keep this page updated. Any significant changes will be noted here, and we’ll notify you via the site banner. Last reviewed: July 2026.

📬 For any cookie‑related questions, feel free to reach out — we’re happy to clarify.
